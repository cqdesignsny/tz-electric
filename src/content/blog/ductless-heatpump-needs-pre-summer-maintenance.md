## How Ductless Heat Pumps Work

Ductless heat pumps operate by transferring heat between an indoor air-handling unit and an outdoor compressor. In cooling mode, the system extracts heat from indoor air and releases it outside, effectively lowering indoor temperatures. These systems are connected by a conduit that houses the power cable, refrigerant tubing, and a condensate drain.

Homeowners in Catskill value mini-splits for their ability to control the temperature in individual rooms, their quiet operation, and their space-saving design. Whether cooling a single room or managing temperatures across multiple zones, ductless systems offer reliable, energy-conscious comfort.

## Risks of Skipping Pre-Summer Maintenance

Neglecting your ductless system before summer can result in reduced efficiency, system strain, and uncomfortable indoor temperatures during peak heat. Here are some common risks:

- **Clogged filters and coils**: These can restrict airflow and force the system to work harder, consuming more energy and raising your utility bills.
- **Blocked condensate drains**: A blocked drain can lead to moisture issues and even water damage or mold growth.
- **Refrigerant leaks**: Low refrigerant levels reduce performance and can harm the environment if leaks go undetected.
- **Faulty controls or sensors**: These may cause inconsistent cooling or prevent the system from responding to temperature changes properly.

These issues often go unnoticed until it's too late. Taking the time now to perform seasonal maintenance helps avoid costly emergency service calls later.

## Key Maintenance Tasks for Your Ductless Heat Pump

To keep your system running efficiently, focus on these essential tasks:

- **Clean or replace air filters**: Clogged filters reduce system airflow and cooling efficiency.
- **Wipe down coils**: Clean coils allow better heat exchange and improve overall performance.
- **Clear the condensate drain**: Ensure it's free of debris to avoid moisture problems and system shutdowns.
- **Check refrigerant levels**: Low levels often indicate a leak that must be repaired by a professional.
- **Test system controls**: Ensure the thermostat is accurate and that your system responds to commands correctly.

While homeowners can handle many of these tasks, a professional service ensures nothing is missed.
