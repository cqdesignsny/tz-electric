## Key Spring Maintenance Tasks

Maintaining your standby generator effectively involves a few key tasks that should be part of your spring routine. Begin with inspecting and cleaning the unit thoroughly. Clear away any dirt, leaves, or debris that may have accumulated around the generator over the winter months. This ensures optimal airflow and prevents potential blockages that can lead to overheating and reduced efficiency.

Next, check and replace the oil and air filters. These components are crucial to the smooth operation of your generator. Clean filters ensure that the engine runs efficiently and keep the generator in top condition. Examine the battery and charging system as well. A weak or dead battery is one of the most common reasons generators fail to start. Make sure the battery is fully charged, and consider replacing it if it's more than a few years old.

Don't forget to examine fuel lines for any signs of leaks or damage. Replace brittle or cracked lines to prevent fuel from seeping out, which can pose both a safety hazard and affect the generator's performance. Finally, running a full system test is essential. This allows you to check the generator's functionality, identify any issues, and address them promptly.
