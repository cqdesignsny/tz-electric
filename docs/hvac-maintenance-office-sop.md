# HVAC Maintenance Price Book — Office SOP

How to quote mini-split and ducted maintenance jobs in Housecall Pro.

## Purpose of this guide

Our maintenance price book is built modular. You build a quote one component at a time, the same way the system was installed. There are no pre-built bundles. This keeps quoting accurate, transparent for the customer, and makes sure we hit our $230/hr target rate on every job.

This SOP walks through how to identify what the customer has, build the quote correctly in HCP, decide between one-time and contract pricing, and handle on-site upcharges when the tech finds a unit that needs more than a quick clean.

## 1. Understand the structure

Every quote is built from three types of line items. You'll find them all under **Heating and Air Conditioning > HVAC Maintenance** in the price book.

### A. Outdoor unit (always required)

- Outdoor Compressor — Quick Clean (One-Time)
- Outdoor Compressor — Quick Clean (3-Year Contract)

Add ONE per outdoor unit on the system. Most homes have one outdoor unit. If a customer has two separate systems, that's two outdoor compressor lines.

### B. Indoor units (one line per indoor unit)

- Indoor High-Wall Head
- Indoor Ceiling Cassette
- Indoor Low-Wall (Floor-Mount) Head
- Indoor Ducted Air Handler

Add ONE line per indoor unit, choosing the type that matches what the customer has. Each type has both a one-time and a 3-year contract version.

### C. Deep clean add-ons (only when needed)

These are NOT added at quote time. The tech adds them on-site if the unit requires more than a standard quick clean (heavy buildup, mold, biological growth, odor). More on that in Section 5.

## 2. Identifying what the customer has

Before you can build the quote, you need to know what they have. Ask the customer these questions, or pull it from the install paperwork if we did the original install.

### Questions to ask

- How many outdoor units does your system have? (Usually one, sometimes two for larger homes.)
- How many indoor units does it have, and what do they look like?
- Are the indoor units mounted on the wall up high, recessed into the ceiling, sitting on the floor, or hidden behind a vent (ducted)?
- Do you have an active maintenance agreement with us? (If not, ask if they want to hear about one. More in Section 4.)

### Quick visual ID guide

- **High-Wall Head** — mounted high on a wall, looks like a long box
- **Ceiling Cassette** — recessed into the ceiling with a square grille
- **Low-Wall (Floor-Mount) Head** — sits on the floor at baseboard level
- **Ducted Air Handler** — not visible, air comes from regular supply vents

When in doubt, ask for a photo. Customers can text or email a quick picture. It takes 30 seconds and prevents quoting errors. If a tech shows up to a cassette job that was quoted as a high-wall, we lose money: the cassette takes 50% longer.

## 3. Building the quote in HCP

In Housecall Pro, when you're creating an estimate or job, search the price book for the components and add them one at a time. Always quote in this order:

1. Add ONE outdoor compressor line.
2. Add the appropriate indoor unit line for each indoor unit on the system.
3. Confirm the quantity on each line matches what the customer has.
4. Choose either the one-time or 3-year contract version of each line (see Section 4).
5. Review the total, send the estimate.

### Examples

These are common configurations. All examples shown at one-time pricing. Subtract roughly 7.5% for 3-year contract pricing.

| 1 outdoor + 2 high-walls | Outdoor x1, High-Wall x2 | $328 |
| 1 outdoor + 3 high-walls | Outdoor x1, High-Wall x3 | $432 |

## 4. One-time vs. 3-year contract pricing

Every component has two pricing tiers. Picking the right one matters. It affects the quote total and how the job gets coded for reporting.

### Use One-Time pricing when

- The customer is paying for a single visit with no commitment.
- They've declined a maintenance agreement or aren't ready to commit.
- This is a brand new customer who hasn't been offered an agreement yet.

### Use 3-Year Contract pricing when

- The customer has signed an active 3-year maintenance agreement.
- They're signing one as part of this visit.
- Roughly 7.5% lower per visit. The customer's reward for committing.

### Always offer the contract

Every maintenance call is a chance to convert a one-off into a recurring relationship. The contract version saves the customer money over three years and locks in their service. If you're not sure whether to offer it, default to YES. Mention it casually when discussing the quote. Even if they decline this time, they now know it exists.

## 5. Deep clean add-ons (handled on-site)

We quote every job at quick clean pricing. If the tech arrives and finds a unit needs more than a filter wash and coil rinse (heavy biological growth, mold, mildew odor, or buildup that won't rinse off), they'll add a Deep Clean Add-On line item per affected unit.

### Deep clean upcharges per unit

- High-Wall Head: +$69
- Ceiling Cassette: +$98
- Low-Wall Head: +$79
- Ducted Air Handler: +$117

### Office role when this happens

- The tech will call you (or notify through HCP) before adding the deep clean.
- Confirm the customer is OK with the additional charge before approving.
- Update the job in HCP with the deep clean line item(s) so the invoice reflects it.
- If the customer declines the deep clean, the tech will perform the standard quick clean only and note in the job that deep cleaning was recommended but declined.

### Why this matters

If we don't price the deep clean correctly, we eat the extra labor. A high-wall deep clean adds 18 minutes per unit. On a 1:3 system with all three needing deep clean, that's nearly an hour of extra work. The $69-per-unit upcharge keeps us at our target rate.

## 6. Common quoting questions

**"The customer has a 1:3 system but only wants two units cleaned."**

Add 1 outdoor compressor + 2 indoor head lines. Note in the job notes which two units are being serviced. Be aware: the tech may push back if the third unit is on the same line set and shares the drain. Sometimes it's not practical to skip one. Coordinate with the tech if it comes up.

**"They have a ducted system AND a mini-split head off the same outdoor unit."**

This is a hybrid system. Add 1 outdoor compressor + 1 ducted air handler line + 1 line per mini-split head. Bill normally. The outdoor unit is shared but each indoor unit is its own service.

**"The customer is asking for a price over the phone but isn't sure what they have."**

Give a range based on common configurations (e.g. "a typical mini-split maintenance runs from $224 for a single-zone system up to $432 for a three-zone system"), then ask for a photo or schedule a no-charge quick assessment. Don't lock in a price until we know the system.

**"What if they have a brand we don't service?"**

Maintenance is generally brand-agnostic for mini-splits and ducted systems. The cleaning process is the same. Confirm with the tech if it's an unusual or commercial system. We do NOT service window units, portable AC, or commercial rooftop units.

## 8. When to escalate

Send these scenarios to a manager or to the field tech directly before quoting.

- Commercial buildings or systems above 5 tons.
- Customer is asking for a multi-year contract longer than 3 years.
- Customer is requesting service on a system we did not install and you can't confirm what they have from photos.
- Customer is pushing back hard on price. Don't discount on the spot, escalate.
- Anything that doesn't cleanly fit one of the line items in the price book.
