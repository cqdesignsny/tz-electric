## 1. Pricing & Fees

### Field Assessment vs Diagnostic Service

Two on-site appointment types. Use the right one based on the customer's need.

**Field Assessment** is for pricing new work or upgrades. Roughly 30 minutes on-site.
- Local Zone: **$169** (15–20 min from shop)
- Standard Zone: **$239** (25–45 min from shop)
- Extended Zone: **$329** (45 min to 1 hr 15 min)
- Common scope: new fixtures/outlets, dedicated circuits, EV chargers, service upgrades, generators, mini-split electrical, planned upgrades.
- Larger or multi-item projects can be forwarded to the estimating team for a detailed proposal.
- The assessment fee may be credited or waived depending on the project.

**Diagnostic Service** is for existing problems, failures, troubleshooting.
- Level 1 Diagnostic starts at **$244**.
- Level 2 / Level 3 may be recommended on-site for advanced troubleshooting; reviewed and approved with the customer before proceeding.
- Common scope: power loss, breaker trips, lighting failures, equipment malfunctions, intermittent issues, unknown wiring problems.

### Priority & Emergency Rates

**In-Hours Priority Service** (during normal business hours, urgent jobs):
- Priority Dispatch Fee: **$275**, includes priority scheduling and dispatch.
- On-site, technician quotes any repair before proceeding.

**After-Hours Emergency Service** (evenings, weekends, select holidays):
- Emergency Dispatch Fee: **$475**, includes travel + up to 1 hour of on-site diagnostic/troubleshooting.
- After the initial diagnostic period, technician reviews findings and quotes any repair before continuing.

### Trip Charge Outside Standard Zones

**$75** trip charge for jobs outside the standard zones.

**$180–$380** to replace an existing outlet or switch.

### EV Charger Install Range

Most residential EV charging installs: **$900–$4,000+**.

**Standard EV outlet install (near main panel):** starts around **$950**. Includes: dedicated EV-rated receptacle, 60A breaker (when panel allows), correctly sized copper conductors, standard accessible wiring path, basic testing and code-compliant connection.

**Pricing increases for:** longer panel-to-charger runs, trenching to detached garages, opening/repairing finished walls, limited attic/crawlspace/basement access, full or undersized panels needing upgrades, hardwired chargers, permit/inspection requirements, complex routing.

**Best value:** charger close to panel with clear access lands at the low end. Trenching, panel upgrades, or difficult routing pushes to the high end. Recommend on-site Field Assessment for accurate pricing on detached garages, long runs, or service upgrades.

### Hot Tub & Pool Wiring

**Hot tub (residential):** $2,500–$5,500. Trenching/excavation typically NOT included unless specifically quoted.
- Variables: distance from panel, panel capacity, wiring path/access, disconnect placement, GFCI requirements, surface vs concealed, detached structures.

**Pool wiring:** $4,500–$15,000+.
- Variables: above-ground vs in-ground, pool type (gunite, vinyl, fiberglass), pump/filter equipment, heat pump/heater connections, salt/chlorination systems, underwater lighting, auto cover, landscape lighting, subpanels/feeders, bonding/grounding requirements, trenching.
- Strict safety and bonding requirements; every installation must be reviewed individually. On-site Field Assessment strongly recommended for in-ground pools or remote hot tubs.

### Generator Transfer Switch Only

**Manual transfer switch (typically with portable generators):** $1,200–$2,500. Variables: number of circuits backed up, distance to panel, panel space/access, wiring path.

**Automatic Transfer Switch (ATS, used with Generac standby):** $2,500–$4,000+. Variables: existing service config, meter-to-panel rewiring, underground vs overhead, generator location/feeder distance, grounding/bonding updates, whole-home vs selective load, permits.

On-site Field Assessment recommended.

### Whole-Home Surge Protection

**Range:** $450–$1,200+.

**Entry-level (~$450):** single standard panel, basic suppression, standard manufacturer warranty.

**Premium (~$1,200):** enhanced suppression capacity, multi-panel coverage, higher joule ratings/faster response, stronger warranties, connected equipment protection plans up to **$50,000** in covered damage (varies by manufacturer/model).

**Variables:** number of panels, services/meter locations, main panel brand, desired warranty tier, standard vs premium device, labor for difficult access.

**Why it matters:** protects HVAC/mini-splits, appliances, TVs/electronics, computers/networking, smart home, generator controls, transfer switches.

## 2. Warranty, Brands & Payment

### Labor Warranty

Standard workmanship warranty: **1 year** on TZ-installed labor, normal use conditions.

### Mini-Split Warranty (Mitsubishi)

Qualifying new ductless installs: **12-year equipment + 12-year labor** (Mitsubishi Electric, installed per manufacturer guidelines).

Eligibility for the 12-year labor warranty requires:
- Warranty service must be performed by TZ Electric.
- Proper routine maintenance and care.
- Filters, coils, drains, related components maintained as recommended.
- Operated under normal intended use.

Exclusions: negligence, abuse, lack of maintenance, environmental damage, unauthorized repairs, misuse.

### Manufacturer Warranties (Other Equipment)

**TZ handles registration** for qualifying installations and provides documentation.

**Generators (Generac):** registered with manufacturer, customer receives confirmation directly from Generac.

**Mini-splits (Mitsubishi):** TZ completes registration on customer's behalf.

**Other (hybrid water heaters, smart devices/controls, exhaust fans, customer-supplied):** subject to manufacturer's standard terms. Example: Ruud hybrid water heater carries roughly **7-year** manufacturer warranty depending on model.

### Signature Plan Extended Warranty

**No.** Signature Plan members do NOT receive an extended warranty.

### HVAC Brands Beyond Mitsubishi

Primarily Mitsubishi Electric for ductless and heat pump systems. After working with multiple brands, TZ has standardized on Mitsubishi for long-term reliability, cold-climate performance, efficiency, parts/manufacturer support. The agent should reinforce this as a deliberate quality choice, not a limitation.

### Water Heater Brands

Primarily **Ruud and Rheem**. Standard electric tanks, hybrid heat pump, power vent, high-efficiency, larger capacity residential systems.

### Payment Methods

- Credit / debit cards
- Checks
- ACH / bank transfer
- Cash
- Wisetack financing
- Synchrony financing

### Deposits

**Single-day / single-stage installs** (service upgrades, panel replacements, smaller generator projects): 50% deposit at scheduling, 50% on completion.

**Mini-split installs:** 50% / 50%.

**Standby generator installs:** 50% / 50% (deposit at scheduling/equipment ordering).

**Rough-in / new construction / full home wiring:** 35% at project start, 35% at rough-in completion, 25% at final trim-out, 10% at final certificates / closeout.

**Field Assessments & Diagnostics:** payment collected on-site at time of service.

**Credit card on file required when:**
- Homeowner not present during appointment.
- Same-day install with no one home.
- Field Assessment / Diagnostic with no on-site payer.

The card on file gets charged once approved service is completed.

## 3. Emergency Triage & On-Call

### What Counts as a True Emergency (escalate immediately)

- Gas leak (always 911 first)
- Sparking or smoking panel / outlet
- No heat below threshold temperature
- Burst pipe actively flooding
- Total power loss to home
- No hot water (winter)
- Generator failure during outage

### No-Heat Emergency Threshold

**Below 32°F outside.**

### Tyler's Notification Preference

**Ping on every after-hours dispatch**, not just serious ones.

### After-Hours Emergency Dispatch SOP

**Initial customer intake:** confirm name, service address, best phone, description of issue, safety concerns (sparking, burning smell, no heat, flooding, full outage), whether anyone vulnerable is home, and customer approval of after-hours pricing/dispatch fees.

**Standard dispatch (6:00 AM–10:00 PM):**
1. Notify on-call supervisor by text with full call details.
2. Contact on-call technician by text and phone.
3. If no response, retry every 15 minutes until acknowledged or reassigned.

**Overnight (10:00 PM–6:00 AM):**
1. One immediate notification to on-call technician.
2. Notify on-call supervisor.
3. If no response, resume follow-ups starting 6:00 AM.

**Morning follow-up (6:00 AM–7:30 AM):** retry on-call tech every 30 minutes.

**Transition:** at 7:30 AM, after-hours escalation ends and call moves into normal business-hour dispatch.

**Customer communication standard:** keep customer informed throughout. Calm, professional, clear that the request is received and active efforts are underway.

### After-Hours Non-Emergency SOP

For non-urgent after-hours calls, reassure, collect info, schedule follow-up.

**Customer script:**
> "Thank you for calling TZ Electric. Based on what you've described, this does not appear to require emergency after-hours dispatch. What we're going to do now is gather your information, and a member of our office team will reach out as soon as normal business hours begin. We'll review your needs and do our best to accommodate same-day service whenever possible, depending on scheduling, seasonality, and urgency. We will follow up with you to discuss the best options moving forward."

**Collect:** name, address, phone, email (if available), brief description, preferred callback time, whether any no-heat / no-hot-water / no-power signal, scheduling limits for next day.

**Internal notification:** immediately text the during-hours office point of contact with customer name, address, phone, time of call, nature of request, urgency notes, callback availability.

**Automated morning follow-up:**
- 7:00 AM: first reminder text to office contact with customer details.
- 7:30 AM: final reminder text.

**Next-day priority order:** no heat / no cooling (seasonal), no hot water, partial power loss, active leaks / equipment concerns, then standard service / estimates.

## 4. Existing vs New Customer Behavior

### Existing Customer Lookup

When a phone number or customer record matches an existing customer:
- Identify the customer profile.
- Greet by name.
- Confirm how to help today.

**Standard greeting:** "Hi [Customer Name], thank you for calling TZ Electric, Plumbing, Heating, and Cooling. How may I help you today?"

### Privacy & Data Access Rules

For standard incoming calls:
- ✓ Look up customer name and basic account match.
- ✗ Do NOT review or reference prior job history unless required.
- ✗ Do NOT volunteer past service details.
- ✗ Do NOT discuss prior invoices, pricing, repairs, notes, or technician comments.
- Keep focus on the current request only.

### Appointment Status Questions

Allowed to share: upcoming appointment date, time window, status (scheduled / pending / en route if available), reschedule or confirm attendance.

Not allowed: prior appointment dates, previous job notes, past pricing, prior repairs, technician notes, old invoices/balances unless specifically routed to office.

### Past Job / Invoice Calls

AI handles "when is my appointment" and basic status. **Escalates** billing disputes and historical record requests to office.

**Suggested escalation script:**
> "I'd be happy to help with that. For previous job records and detailed account history, one of our office team members will be the best person to assist you. I'll make sure they receive your request and follow up."

## 5. Commercial, Rentals, Home Warranties

### Commercial Trades TZ Takes

- Electrical
- HVAC / mini-split
- Generator
- Hot water heater

### Commercial Inquiry Routing

**Immediate transfer to live agent.** Commercial customers need fast coordination, custom pricing, scheduling logistics, tenant/PM communication, and project-specific decisions. Don't keep them in a standard automated workflow longer than necessary.

**Trigger immediate transfer if caller is:** business owner, property manager, GC, builder/developer, facility manager, restaurant/retail/office owner, municipal/school rep, multi-unit housing rep, landlord with commercial property, existing commercial account.

**Common commercial requests:** service calls for businesses, no power / urgent outages, HVAC failures in occupied buildings, plumbing affecting operations, generator service, tenant fit-outs, remodels/additions, preventative maintenance, multi-location, bidding/estimates, recurring service.

**AI script:**
> "Thank you for calling TZ Electric, Plumbing, Heating, and Cooling. I understand this is a commercial inquiry. Let me connect you with a live team member who can best assist you right away."

**If no live agent available:** collect company name, caller, callback number, address, nature of issue, business impact (closed / no heat / no power / leak / emergency), best callback time. Notify office immediately.

**High Priority** if affecting operations: no power, no heat, active leak, safety, shutdown risk.

### Renter / Tenant Calls

**Require landlord approval before booking. Soft-block at the point of capture.**

The form / AI asks "Are you the homeowner or are you renting?" upfront. If renting, an extra step collects landlord name, phone, and email, plus a confirmation: "I have my landlord's permission for this work / they've authorized the visit."

The submission posts to the standard lead pipeline (see section 11) tagged `Renter - Verify with Landlord`. The office calls the landlord before booking. AI / form **never auto-books a renter without office sign-off.**

For voice and SMS specifically, Claire branches early: "Got it. Before we can book, our team will need to verify with your landlord. Can I get their name and best phone number?"

### Home Warranty Companies (AHS, Choice, First American, etc.)

**No.** TZ does not work directly with home warranty companies. Decline warm, pivot to financing — never bash the warranty company.

**Decline script:**

> "We don't work directly with home warranty companies. We've found we can serve our customers faster and with higher quality working direct. We'd still love to help you out, and if cost is a concern we offer financing through Wisetack and Synchrony with quick approvals. Would you like me to set up a Field Assessment or a Diagnostic visit?"

The pivot to financing is intentional: customers reach out to warranty companies because they're cost-sensitive, and a flat decline without an alternative loses the lead.

## 7. Scheduling & Handoffs

### Job Assignment by Skill Tag

Technicians have skill tags. Assignment must match the appointment type.

**Field Assessment / Diagnostic appointments** (any tech with `Field Assessment` or `Diagnostic` tag):
- Field Assessment visits
- Troubleshooting / Diagnostic calls
- Standard service evaluations
- Basic repair visits

**Estimate appointments require trade-specific tag:**
- HVAC tag → heating, cooling, mini-splits, heat pumps, ducted systems
- Plumbing tag → plumbing estimates, water heaters, piping, drainage, fixtures
- Electrical tag → panel upgrades, generators, EV chargers, rewires, service upgrades, lighting

**Multi-trade requests** (generator + propane + electrical, mini-split + panel upgrade, water heater + electrical, plumbing + electrical remodel): route to most appropriate estimator or office coordinator for manual review.

**No correct tag available:** do not auto-assign incorrectly. Hold for office review and notify scheduling coordinator.

### Arrival Windows

Typically within **30 minutes** of scheduled appointment time when possible.

## 11. Lead Routing into Housecall Pro

Every channel — website form, SMS Claire, voice Claire, web-chat Claire — funnels leads through the same backend so routing is identical and the office sees new work in three coordinated places: the customer's HCP record (as a new estimate), HCP Job Inbox > "API Leads" (as a fresh lead card), and the TZ Switchboard Lead Pipeline (as the office-facing mirror).

**Server-side flow (`src/app/api/leads/submit/route.ts`, also called by the agents' `create_lead_with_estimate` tool):**

1. Persist to Neon (`tz_leads`) first so the lead is recorded even if HCP is unreachable.
2. Find existing customer in HCP by **any of phone, email, or name** (the three lookups fire in parallel; first hit wins, with phone preferred over email over name). Catches the "same person, mistyped name" / "shared household email" / "new phone" cases that phone-only would miss.
3. If found: keep `customer.notes` untouched (it holds persistent customer info like "don't wear shoes in the house" — never overwritten by lead data). The estimate's private notes record which signal matched (`Matched existing customer by: phone|email|name`) so the office can sanity-check.
4. If not found: create a new customer with name, phone, email, address only. `notes` is left blank for the same reason.
5. **Create an unscheduled estimate** against that customer (`work_status: needs scheduling`). Job-specific lead details go in the estimate option's office-internal notes (added via a follow-up `POST /estimates/{eid}/options/{oid}/notes` call). Tags carry service / urgency / scope / flags on the option row so the office can triage at a glance.
6. **Drop a Job Inbox card** via `POST /leads` with top-level `customer_id` referencing the same customer (no duplicate). Same triage tags go on the inbox card so the office spots new leads instantly in HCP's "API Leads" channel and can click through to the customer's record.
7. Stitch `hcp_customer_id`, `hcp_estimate_id`, `hcp_lead_id`, `hcp_customer_existing` back onto the `tz_leads` row so the TZ Switchboard Lead Pipeline can deep-link and the office can confirm the routing worked.
8. Email the office with the same details via Resend regardless of HCP outcome.

**Where each piece of data lands in HCP:**

| Data | HCP location |
|---|---|
| Name, phone, email | `customer` (created or matched by phone) |
| Service address | `customer.addresses` on first creation; estimate-level `address` on every estimate |
| Qualification answers | Estimate `private_notes` (formatted with section headers) |
| Customer notes (free text from form) | Estimate `private_notes` only — NOT customer profile |
| Tags (`Service:`, `Urgency:`, scope, flag tags) | Top-level `tags` on the estimate |
| GCLID / UTM / referral source | Estimate `private_notes` (Attribution section) |

**What customer.notes is reserved for** — and what it's NOT for:
- ✓ Persistent identity / accommodation info ("don't wear shoes in the house", "no longer serviced", "reach via wife's phone")
- ✗ Job-specific details (those live on the estimate)
- ✗ Lead source / GCLID / UTM (those live on the estimate)

**Tag pattern on estimates** (high-signal, surfaced in HCP estimate row):
- `Web Form` (or `TZ AI Agent (SMS)` / `Voice` / `Chat` once those agents ship)
- `Service: <label>`
- `Urgency: <answer>`
- Scope answer if present
- `Existing customer` if HCP returned a match
- Flag tags: `Renter - Verify with Landlord`, `Medical Equipment in Home`, `ACTIVE LEAK`, `Google Ads`

**TZ Switchboard mirror** (`/switchboard/lead-pipeline`): reads from `tz_leads` (Neon), one row per submission. Each row deep-links to the matching HCP estimate via `hcp_estimate_id`. If HCP sync errored mid-flight, the row shows an "HCP sync error" badge with the error message and instructions for the office to recreate manually.

**Two-way status sync.** The office flips estimates Won / Lost in HCP. Every time the Lead Pipeline page loads, the server reads the latest status for any rows whose `estimate_status_synced_at` is older than 5 minutes (capped at 30 rows per request, sequential reads to stay under HCP's rate limit) and writes the categorized status (`open` / `won` / `lost`) back onto `tz_leads.estimate_status`. The page header shows when the sync last ran and exposes a "Refresh statuses" button that bypasses the throttle. Won leads get an emerald accent, lost leads dim to muted, sync errors flag the row.

**For SMS / voice / chat agents:** call the same `POST /api/leads/submit` once qualification is done, with the `qualification` map matching the keys defined in section 6's "Canonical Lead Intake Question Set." The endpoint will handle the customer + estimate routing the same way the website form does, so all channels stay consistent.
